Pro Se Bankruptcy in Missouri: A State Guide

How pro se bankruptcy applies specifically in Missouri bankruptcy cases. Covers Western District of Missouri (Kansas City) and Eastern District (St. Louis). Updated 2026-03-29.

Missouri Bankruptcy Overview

Missouri has two federal bankruptcy districts: the Western District (Kansas City, Springfield, St. Joseph) and the Eastern District (St. Louis, Cape Girardeau). Each district has its own local rules and practices.

Missouri Exemptions

Missouri allows debtors to choose between federal and state exemptions. Key Missouri exemptions include:

  • Homestead: Up to $15,000 equity in your home (Mo. Rev. Stat. 513.475)
  • Vehicle: Up to $3,000 in one motor vehicle (Mo. Rev. Stat. 513.430(3))
  • Wildcard: Up to $600 plus $350 per dependent (Mo. Rev. Stat. 513.430(3))
  • Retirement: Tax-exempt retirement accounts fully exempt

Pro Se Bankruptcy in the Western District

The Western District of Missouri, where Kansas City cases are filed, has specific practices regarding pro se bankruptcy. Judge Cynthia A. Norton and other judges in the district have established precedents that affect how these rules are applied locally.

Key Statistics

Based on Federal Judicial Center data for Missouri:

  • Missouri ranks among the top states for Chapter 13 filings per capita
  • The Western District processes thousands of bankruptcy cases annually
  • Chapter 13 dismissal rates vary significantly by attorney

Finding Help in Missouri

If you need assistance with bankruptcy in Missouri, consider:

  • Legal Aid of Western Missouri (free legal services for qualifying individuals)
  • The Missouri Bar Lawyer Referral Service
  • The U.S. Bankruptcy Court self-help resources
